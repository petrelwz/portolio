Even though online space is great for people to access multiple points of views on certain issues, people tend to side with the opinion they already agree with and accept things that fit in with their belief systems. This is called confirmation bias. Current social media platforms, such as Twitter, reinforce such confirmation bias.

For college students who are in a stage to develop their critical thinking skills, the following application "Critical Thinking Community" is designed based on research in order to help train their critical thinking skills.

Project Goal

After reviewed more than 50 research papers on critical thinking and informal learning, it's important for the students to achieve the following goal.

Competitive Analysis

To understand current critical thinking learning methods

Compared to other critical thinking apps e.g. Critical Thinking Basic, Think-O-Meter and traditional offline debate training, "Critical Thinking Community" allows students to get exposed to various opinions, share and express opposing ideas on a daily base.

User Research

To understand how does social media influence students' information explosure and opinion formation

Through interviewing 6 college students on how they use and interact on Twitter and Quora, to understand their behaviors and motivations in their context.

Insights
  1. People’s judgements of others’ opinions are affected by who the person is, how close they are and what kind of tone they use.
  2. People’s motivation on providing or supporting an opinion or supporting is related to how much they are familiar with the topic and how much they are interested in the topic
